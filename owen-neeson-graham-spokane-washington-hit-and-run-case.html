Spokane Washington hit and run case details

According to a police news release, authorities were called to Lyons Avenue and Division Street at around 7 a.m. on June 23 in response to an incident.

The police allege that an 18-year-old driver, Owen Neeson-Graham, struck a woman and her young son as they were crossing Division Street in a marked crosswalk.

The woman was pushing a stroller with her baby while walking alongside her 4-year-old son.

After the collision, the driver reportedly fled the scene in the vehicle. The woman sustained serious but non-life-threatening injuries.

Fortunately, the baby was unharmed. Video footage of the incident and witness statements aided the police in identifying and locating Neeson-Graham, who was subsequently arrested on charges of felony hit-and-run and vehicular assault.

Neeson-Graham is currently held in Spokane County Jail on a $70,000 bond. He made his initial court appearance on Thursday and is scheduled for arraignment on July 11.

It is worth noting that Owen is also facing multiple assault charges from a previous incident in September in northeast Spokane, with a trial set for November.

The police are requesting the public’s assistance in locating the 2005 Dodge Magnum involved in the crash.

Anyone with information is urged to contact Crime Check at (509) 456-2233, referencing incident number 2023-20122042.
